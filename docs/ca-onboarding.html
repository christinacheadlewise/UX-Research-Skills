Wise is expanding into a Current Account to diversify revenue. This research addresses knowledge gaps in CA onboarding, discoverability, and perceived value within users' broader financial ecosystems.

Branch Pop-Up

High service quality, low product understanding

Customers were low-intent (came for matcha) — excellent staff pitches drove conversions
In-store materials were ineffective; no demo devices for product education
Staff couldn't answer detailed competitor-comparison questions
Detailed product knowledge was lacking and inaccessible

Branch Pop-Up: Recommendations

1. Tighten selling propositions for those new to digital banking
2. Create dedicated 'feature areas' staffed by product teams
3. Provide demos and 'five-minute' pitch sessions for product overview

Principle of Least Astonishment

Users can't find what they're looking for, don't know what to look for, and don't realise things work differently at Wise.

  • Wise perceived as remittance-only — CA features confuse users
  • Multi-currency mechanics cause anxiety ("what happens if I run out?")
  • Multiple cards model doesn't land with everyday spenders
  • "Assets" and "earn a return" miss mental models — users expect "savings" and "interest"
  • Navigation unintuitive; key features hidden in wrong categories

UX Recommendations

1. Offer better guidance for products that break the traditional banking mental model
2. Separate everyday banking features from travel and remittance in navigation

Critical Gap

Wise is not serving Everyday Optimisers — the segment with highest loyalty potential. They need money management tools we don't yet offer competitively.

The domestic everyday spender has a fractured and diverse financial ecosystem curated over time. Wise's position differs by type.

Strategic Recommendations

1. Personalisation — Move away from one-size-fits-all. Help users identify features for their unique needs.
2. Additional Features — Build money management tools to anchor Everyday Optimisers. Offer cashback/rewards to attract Strategic Maximisers.
3. Clarity and Guidance — Reframe terminology ("interest" not "returns"). Explain multi-currency mechanics. Separate everyday banking from remittance.
