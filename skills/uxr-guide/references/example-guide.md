# Example: ARTS Understand Study Discussion Guide (90 min)

This is the structural pattern from Christina's previous generative study — a 90-minute session with multiple exercises and stimulus materials.

## Structure Pattern

1. **Intro & Logistics (5 min)**
   - Consent, recording permission
   - "No right/wrong answers"
   - Brief participant intro

2. **Warm-Up: Current Financial Life (10 min)**
   - Objective: Understand their financial world before introducing Wise
   - "Tell me about the bank accounts and financial apps you use day-to-day"
   - "How do you decide which account to use for what?"
   - Probes: joint accounts, savings, number of providers, satisfaction

3. **Core Section 1: Mental Models (20 min)**
   - Objective: How they categorise financial products
   - Card sort exercise with financial product names
   - "Group these in whatever way makes sense to you"
   - **[Moderator: don't prompt categories — let them create their own]**
   - Follow-up: "Tell me about your groupings"
   - Probes: where does Wise fit? What's it similar to?

4. **Core Section 2: Stimulus Reaction (20 min)**
   - Objective: Test comprehension of value proposition
   - **[Show prototype / screenshots]**
   - "What do you think this is offering?"
   - "What would you expect to be able to do?"
   - "Anything confusing or unexpected?"
   - Probes: terminology, feature comprehension, pricing expectations

5. **Core Section 3: Diary Debrief (25 min)**
   - Objective: Deep-dive on behaviours logged during diary period
   - "Looking at your diary entries, tell me about [specific entry]"
   - "You mentioned [X] — what was happening that day?"
   - **[Moderator: reference specific diary entries prepared in advance]**
   - Probes: context, emotions, alternatives considered, friction points

6. **Wrap-Up (5 min)**
   - "If you could change one thing about how you manage your money, what would it be?"
   - "Anything we didn't cover that you think is important?"
   - Thank, incentive info, next steps

7. **Buffer (5 min)**
   - Built into Core Section 3 — cut last question if running over

## Key Patterns to Replicate

- Every section has an explicit objective
- Main questions are open-ended, probes dig deeper
- Exercises have clear moderator instructions
- Timing is explicit and sections have contingency (what to cut if over)
- Stimulus introduction is marked with moderator notes
- Warm-up starts broad (their world) before narrowing to the research topic
